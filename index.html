Asian Carp

Invasive Species Project

Species Information

Common Name: Asian Carp

Scientific Names:

  • Silver Carp (Hypophthalmichthys molitrix)
  • Bighead Carp (Hypophthalmichthys nobilis)
  • Grass Carp (Ctenopharyngodon idella)
  • Black Carp (Mylopharyngodon piceus)

Fast-growing, eat plankton, and jump when disturbed. They were introduced in the 1970s and have spread rapidly.

Life Cycle & Role

Mature in 2–6 years, live up to 20, lay millions of eggs, and feed on plankton (primary consumers).

Ecological Impact

  • Outcompete native fish for food (plankton).
  • Disrupt aquatic food chains.
  • Harm biodiversity and commercial fisheries.
  • Silver carp leap into boats, injuring humans.

Food Web Disruption

Compete with:

  • Paddlefish
  • Minnows
  • Zooplankton & phytoplankton feeders
  • Birds like Herons
  • Top predators (e.g., Bass, Pike)

Control Efforts

  • Electric barriers in key rivers
  • Commercial harvesting and netting
  • Transport bans across jurisdictions
  • Public awareness and education campaigns
  • Experimental: pheromone traps, genetic control

Effectiveness & Risk

Barriers slow the spread, but risk remains. Great Lakes invasion would threaten a $7B fishing industry and native biodiversity.

Fun Facts

  • Silver Carp can jump over 3 meters high!
  • They're featured in viral boat videos.
  • In Asia, they're a nutritious and valued food.
